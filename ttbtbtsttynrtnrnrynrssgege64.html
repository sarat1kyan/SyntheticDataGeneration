The Problem

Creating custom datasets for niche computer vision tasks is slow, inefficient, and sometimes impossible due to lack of data.

This is especially true when the objects or situations you care about don't exist in public datasets or the internet in general. Taking photos is hard when the subject is rare, private, or doesn't exist in the real world. Annotation makes it worse: teams still label images manually or outsource it, which is slow and doesn't scale.

We ran into this problem while building the object detection system for military equipment. We couldn't find real images, and even if we could - annotating the images was extremely slow for the circumstances. That's what pushed us to build something better.

Solution - What We've Built within 3 weeks

We generate high-quality synthetic datasets automatically using 3D models. The system takes in any 3D asset and outputs labeled images with bounding boxes - ready for training in frameworks like YOLO or any other computer vision model. The system generates Bounding Boxes as well as alpha Masks to be used in both Object Detection and Segmentation.

Scenes are rendered with high visual realism, including detailed lighting, environmental conditions, materials, and camera angles. Everything is randomized to maximize generated dataset diversity.

Unlike generative models, our approach is fast, controllable, and cheap. The user uploads one or more 3D models, selects a few parameters such as the scale of the object, and we handle the rest - no manual labeling, no expensive compute. It takes minutes instead of days, and works even when the real data doesn't exist.

Broader Impact & Vision

While we initially focused on edge cases, our technique is applicable to virtually any scenario that requires annotated synthetic data:

  • Supporting training for perception tasks (object detection, segmentation)
  • Helping to reduce costs associated with real-world data collection and labeling
  • Rapidly prototyping controlled environments for training ML agents
  • Providing extensive and rare datasets for robotics training and simulations
  • Generating multi-angle, labeled data under different conditions that enhances semantic understanding in transformer-based models

Our method provides flexible and precise datasets, whether the goal is routine warehouse robot training or testing models against rare visual anomalies.

Our Next Steps

Our roadmap for advancing synthetic data generation

1. Train on Pure Synthetic Data

Evaluate the effectiveness of training models solely on synthetic datasets, especially for underrepresented or hard-to-capture objects. Compare the results with entirely real datasets training.

2. Mixed Dataset Training

Benchmark the best performance when synthetic data is combined with different size samples of real-world images (find the best combination ratio).

3. Transformer Evaluation

Test how our multi-view, structured datasets improve performance in transformer-based detectors (e.g., DETR, DINO). We believe our datasets will be more effective using transformer-based models.

4. GPU Optimization

Run dataset generation on Tensor Core GPU (A100) to get use of accelerated matrix multiplication (crucial point for us).

5. Domain-Specific Packs

Create and release a high-quality dataset pack focused on a niche real-world scenario.

6. OpenUSD Compatibility

Explore converting our generated scenes to OpenUSD format to assess early compatibility with Omniverse and Replicator workflows.
